## Release interpretation

The DAE boundary family is **reopened and not yet locally release-complete**. The July 20–21 Binaryen-v131 matrices, artifact validity/idempotence, accepted medians, exact-once placement, and CI-profile gate remain strong evidence checkpoints. They predate the July 22–23 lifecycle migrations and cannot close rows that still permit producer-specific mutation tails, fragmented result/type finalization, incomplete specialized/nested diagnostics or invalidation ownership.

One non-DAE residual remains visible: the shared nested local-cleanup roster leaves DAEO canonical output `4,318` bytes larger than Binaryen on the retained artifact even though raw Starshine is `70,900` bytes smaller and plain DAE canonical output is `9,670` bytes smaller. The full large O4z wall-time stop is likewise pre-DAEO in `simplify-locals-nostructure`. Neither is hidden as a Starshine win or a DAE semantic deferral. After the remaining lifecycle rows close, rebuild the native binary and renew the required regular, dedicated aggregate, random-all, wasm-smith, validity, idempotence, artifact, and performance evidence before changing L3 back to closed.
